## EXP-006: REFRESH Radius Gradient (v3, CEM=50, seed=42, 500k ticks)

| R | Exp Pop | Exp REFRESH% | Exp DIVIDE% | Ctrl REFRESH% |
|---|---------|-------------|------------|---------------|
| 1 | 7.5 | 0.0 | 11.1 | 14.2 |
| 2 | 7.5 | 0.0 | 11.1 | 14.2 |
| 3 | 8.3 | 1.5 | 10.3 | 14.2 |
| 5 | 12.8 | 11.5 | 8.9 | 14.2 |
| 8 | 4.0 | 18.4 | 0.0 | 14.2 |

R controls strictness of operational closure:
- **R=1,2**: REFRESH abandoned (0%), pure r-selection (high DIVIDE)
- **R=3**: Maximum behavioral divergence from control
- **R=5**: Balanced maintenance/reproduction
- **R=8**: High maintenance (18%), no reproduction (0%)
- **Control**: Identical across all R values (no freshness pressure)

## Summary of Key Findings

1. **Operational closure is measurable**: Freshness decay creates a quantifiable behavioral signature — REFRESH retention under positive selection vs drift
2. **E_MAX amplifies the effect**: Energy cap forces organisms to allocate instruction cycles to maintenance, making the exp/ctrl difference larger
3. **Cell v3 produces strategy diversification**: Per-cell freshness creates diverse maintenance strategies (REFRESH 1-24%) that global freshness does not
4. **R parameter controls the r/K selection continuum**: Small R = r-selection (abandon maintenance, maximize reproduction). Large R = K-selection (invest in maintenance, reduce reproduction)
5. **Data cells show promise for D2**: Experience-based decisions (store and compare energy readings) improve reproductive efficiency
